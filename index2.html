    Greenhouse Gas Emissions


    • Methane gas released from decomposing food in landfills contributes to climate change and global warming.

    • As global temperatures increase, it causes changes in weather patterns, rising sea levels, and negatively affects ecosystems, wildlife, and human health.

    • Climate change caused by greenhouse gas emissions can have devastating consequences on agriculture, water resources, and human livelihoods.

    Resource Depletion


    • Wasting resources like water and land can lead to the depletion of these natural resources over time.

    • As water resources decrease, it can lead to water scarcity, droughts, and food insecurity in many regions.

    • Overuse of land resources can lead to soil erosion, degradation, and loss of biodiversity, impacting wildlife and ecosystems.

    Environmental Pollution


    • Landfills can take up valuable space, and the pollution created by them can harm wildlife and ecosystems.

    • Transporting and disposing of food waste also produces carbon emissions and contributes to air pollution, which negatively impacts human health and the environment.

    • Contamination of nearby soil and water sources from landfills and food waste can have harmful impacts on plant and animal life, and human health.

    Biodiversity Loss


    • Wasting food can contribute to biodiversity loss, which is the decline in the variety of living organisms in a given area.

    • Biodiversity loss can have cascading effects on ecosystems, such as the loss of pollinators, which can impact food production.

    • Food waste contributes to biodiversity loss by promoting the overuse of natural resources, which can lead to deforestation and habitat loss for wildlife.
